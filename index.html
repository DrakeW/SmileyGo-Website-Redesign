Who We Help

Education

As the umbrella corporation of NGOs, SmileyGo has assisted Yatay Peru, which teaches Andean children music. SmileyGo has empowered NGOs to offer merit-based college scholarships for teenagers in rural Pampas Grandes, Huaraz Music is the world’s language that integrates cultures in our 21st century globe. With Yatay Peru, we have sent twenty volunteers every six months to share our musical knowledge with the kids. As a result, volunteers send videos via YouTube to continue making a difference in this community.

Environment

SmileyGo has stimulated NGOs that focus on reforestation, water management and recycling. As Pampas Grande NGOs accelerator, SmileyGo has triggered the reforestation program in the Peruvian Andes by planting 1000 pine and eucalyptus trees. Today, several NGOs are a part of the sustainability team of SmileyGo as well. Together we can make a difference in our environment starting from here and ending with Peru, Chile and Argentina.

Health

SmileyGo has coached Pan Peru and other NGOs in order to make a sustainable difference in the rural health of Peru, Chile, Argentina and Mexico. We have helped over 12500 citizens in the past eleven years with the help not only from Pan Peru staff, but also from the children of Pampas Grande. Here, we are not only giving the Pampas citizens medical attention, but we are teaching them how to take of themselves through clinics, workshops and CPR conventions. The people receiving our help, are learning from us and hence depending more on themselves in the areas of pediatrics, neurology and dermatology.

Family Formation

Pan Peru is our key player in regards to value formation, family counseling and community outreach in the Peruvian Andes. For the past ten years, SmileyGo has assisted Pan Peru in order to make a more solid impact in the community of Pampas Grande. Pan Peru has taken psychologists, family counselors and psychiatrists to rural Peru in order to promote social welfare, family union and spiritual health. In addition, Pan Peru takes spiritual pastors to teach the kids how to pray, meditate and reflect upon their dreams, goals and acts.

To be a globally recognized platform that connects the resources of for-profit companies and the missions of non-profits in order to foster millions of smiles in the children of underserved communities. To aid in the development of long-term partnerships between the company and the nonprofit in the field of technology, education, women's rights and sustainability

We strategically matchmake corporations with nonprofits in order to foster win-win situations for both sides of the platform. We stimulate sustainable social welfare by connecting the companies' Corporate Social Resonsibility and Philanthropy strategies with the tangible needs of the nonprofit, such as infrastructure, volunteers, and funding. Thus, we will benefit both sides of the platform: by not only leveraging the firm's brand equity, employee motivation, and socially-responsible image, but also the performance of the nonprofits (by providing infrastructure and financial capital) with the kids from underpriveleged communities; hence, creating millions of smiles.

The goals of SmileyGo operations are based on bilateral gain, a win-win situation in which all parties benefit from an interaction.

When SmileyGo connects a company to a non-profit, we trigger a 3-way benefit system.

Non-Profit:

  • Receives tangible resources, such as textbooks or monetary support, from the company. These resources accomplish or contribute to the non-profit's campaign.
  • Gains a potential long-term partnership with a company in fulfilling its mission.

Company:

  • Easily achieves its CSR goal by connecting to a relevant non-profit.
  • Gains improved corporate image and brand awareness by the public when SmileyGo promotes and markets the brand as a socially responsible company.
  • Increases employee retention as employees gain a healthier brand awareness of their own company for being involved in philanthropy.

Employee:

  • Feels a satisfaction of having impacted the society by helping others in need.
  • If working with SmileyGo on an individual level, becomes a leader of a SmileyGo campaign and grows one's social mission to a corporate level.

SmileyGo strengthens the communication between companies and non-profits in the pursuit of a same goal and creates long-lasting business partnerships.

Giving is the foundation of SmileyGo, a social venture founded by Pedro Espinoza, Jamie Rosenstein, Milla-Mari Vastavuo and Joy Ting Zhang on July 4, 2014 to bring smiles to children in socioeconomically under-served communities in the world. SmileyGo connects educational nonprofits to the resources of for-profit companies’ corporate social responsibility (CSR) in order to provide tangible contributions to social welfare.

Pedro was exposed to business operations and development since he was 13 years old, when he started working in Peru as a car mechanic and later as a sales rep. for Toyota. While volunteering at a non-profit organization, Pan Peru Nonprofit, he observed the lack of educational experience and opportunities for children in the rural community of Pampas Grande, which triggered his decision to combine his business knowledge and his desire to serve others. Pedro started his first Peru-based non-profit, Yatay Peru, which provided musical education to the youth in Pampas Grande. After moving to the Bay Area to attend UC Berkeley, he met 3 other people in Stanford’s Technology Entrepreneurship class who had the same desire to fill the opportunity gap globally.

Under the mentorship of Tom Kosnik, Rebeca Hwang, Ernestine Fu, Kaia Simmons, and John Dodini, and through a strong network of personal and professional relationships, SmileyGo launched full steam from the Bay Area and now includes members in 6 continents and 18 countries. From donation drives in the Bay Area to Wealthing Institute’s personal finance textbook donations to Pampas Grande, SmileyGo shows us that it is not only better but also very possible to give to the world, if equipped with the vigor and initiative of like-minded people.

How Do We Improve Education in India?

November 16, 2014 at 12:30PM

Chloe Park

According to Dr. Madhav Chavan, the CEO and Co-founder of the Pratham Organization, India’s education suffers from a severe lack of growth, both in terms of the number of people who are educated and the depth of that education. 50% of children at the primary level of education (up to age 14) have a high chance of not starting or completing secondary education. With 25% of adult Indians being illiterate and 50% being semi-educated, only the select few in a higher socioeconomic class in the country are adequately educated.

Some of the reasons that are causing this less-than-stellar condition of education in India are:

  1. 1. Many children come from illiterate or semi-literate families who might not have the resources or leisure to oversee the children’s education.
  2. 2. There are not enough trained teachers.>
  3. 3. Learning needs to move beyond textbooks. There are many fields that children can explore, such as art, health and sports that are not part of the current educational system in India.

The effectiveness of educational policies and system are under hot debate in any country. We can’t say one country has it all covered. But if SmileyGo wants to provide more educational opportunities to children, the above reasons give us some eye-opening clues to how we can improve education anywhere.

  1. 1. Education doesn’t have to be limited to just children. By working with non-profits or campaigns that teach life skills or job coaching to under-educated parents, we are ultimately creating a better environment in which the children can grow.
  2. 2. We need to constantly improve the education of our teachers if we want them to teach the rights things, most effectively.
  3. 3. Teaching basic reading and math skills is crucial, but an arts-and-craft session or a class on healthy eating habits is equally quality education that will help our children grow into smart and creative adults.

SmileyGo is learning with you, so we are excited to see how we can incorporate these key points in pursuing our mission
